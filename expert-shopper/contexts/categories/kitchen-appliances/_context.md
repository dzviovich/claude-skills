# Category Context: Kitchen Appliances

Parent category for: `espresso-machines/`, `air-fryers/`, `stand-mixers/`, `blenders/`, etc.

## General Kitchen Appliance Buying Principles

- **Counter space is often the binding constraint**: Always ask. A beautiful $1,000 machine
  that doesn't fit on the counter is useless.
- **Noise**: Apartments and thin walls make noise a real concern. Worth asking about.
- **Power / voltage**: High-wattage appliances (espresso machines, blenders) should confirm
  household circuit capacity for very high-draw units.
- **Ease of cleaning**: Often underweighted by buyers, often regretted. Ask how much they
  care about cleanup. A machine with 15 parts to disassemble and hand-wash becomes a burden.
- **Build quality over aesthetics**: Kitchen appliances get daily use for years. Plastic bodies
  are lighter; stainless/metal bodies are more durable. Worth the upcharge for frequent users.
- **Warranty and service**: Brands like Breville, KitchenAid, and De'Longhi have strong US
  service networks. Some European specialty brands have limited US service — flag this.
