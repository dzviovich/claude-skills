# Category Context: Headphones (Over-ear / On-ear Wireless)

Subcategory of: `electronics`
Note: For IEM/earbuds, see `electronics/earbuds/_context.md` if it exists.

## Key Buying Criteria

### Critical questions to ask in intake
- **Form factor**: Over-ear (circumaural) vs. on-ear (supra-aural) vs. true wireless earbuds.
  These are meaningfully different products. Confirm which the user wants.
- **Active Noise Cancellation (ANC)**: Do they specifically want ANC? How important is it?
  (ANC quality varies enormously — from Sony/Bose top-tier to budget-tier that barely works.)
- **Primary use case priority**: Call quality (mic) vs. music listening vs. both equally?
  Some headphones are excellent for music but mediocre mics, and vice versa.
- **Platform / ecosystem**: iPhone, Android, Windows, Mac? Some headphones integrate tightly
  with specific ecosystems (Apple AirPods Max with iOS, Sony with Android, etc.).
  Ask — don't assume based on what they mentioned elsewhere.
- **Connectivity**: Do they need multipoint (connect to 2+ devices simultaneously)?
  e.g., work laptop + phone simultaneously. This is a common pain point.
- **Sound signature preference**: If they have an opinion: bass-heavy, neutral/balanced,
  bright/detailed? Most non-audiophiles just want "good sound" — that's fine too.
- **Comfort / fit**: Some people have large heads and find some headphones uncomfortably tight.
  Long-session comfort (ear pad material: leather vs. fabric; clamping force)?
- **Portability**: Fold flat? Travel case included? How often will they carry them?
- **Battery life needs**: Daily commuter (8–10 hrs fine) vs. frequent flyer (20+ hrs preferred)?

### Key specs to include in comparison table
- ANC quality (excellent / very good / good / fair) — subjective but important; cite sources
- Mic quality (for calls): rated or noted from reviews
- Battery life (hrs with ANC on)
- Multipoint support (yes/no)
- Bluetooth codec support (SBC/AAC/LDAC/aptX) — matters for audiophiles
- Weight (grams) — comfort indicator
- Fold/portable (yes/no)
- Price (new)
- iOS / Android integration notes

### Common pitfalls
- Price ≠ ANC quality in a linear way. Sony WH-1000XM5 and Bose QC45/Ultra are the clear
  ANC leaders; many cheaper options fall far short despite marketing claims.
- "Transparency mode" quality varies — matters for users who commute and need to hear announcements.
- Ear cup material: protein leather looks premium but gets hot and peels after 1–2 years;
  fabric/velour breathes better. Worth mentioning.
- Some "wireless" headphones don't support calling wirelessly from a PC without a USB dongle —
  relevant for remote workers.
- Budget recommendation trap: under $100, ANC quality is generally disappointing. Be honest
  if the user's budget makes a good ANC headphone difficult.
- Apple AirPods Max: excellent quality but $550, no USB-C charging historically, case is odd.
  Mention only as a premium/stretch option.

### ANC Leader Reference (as of 2024–2025)
1. Bose QuietComfort Ultra — best ANC, best comfort, excellent call quality
2. Sony WH-1000XM5 — near-equal ANC, better sound customization, LDAC, multipoint
3. Bose QuietComfort 45 — slightly older, still excellent, often on sale
4. Jabra Evolve2 85 — enterprise/call-focused, outstanding mic
5. Apple AirPods Max — best for iOS, premium price, solid ANC

### Price tiers (as of 2024–2025)
- Budget: $50–$100 (Anker Soundcore Q45, etc.) — decent sound, weak ANC
- Mid: $100–$200 (Sony WH-1000XM4 used, Soundcore Space Q45) — solid
- Premium: $200–$350 (Sony XM5, Bose QC45/Ultra) — best class
- Flagship: $350–$600+ (AirPods Max, Bose QC Ultra with spatial audio)
